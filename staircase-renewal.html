From Start to Finish: Breaking Down Each Phase of Staircase Renewal

Replacing a stair case involves multiple steps. Here’s how it works:

  • Planning: Dimensions are taken, and styles are finalised.
  • Product Preference: Choose wood, metal, or a combination.
  • Manufacturing: Custom staircases require more time, but pre-fabricated options are quicker.
  • Removal and Installation: Your Existing staircase is removed, and the new one is fitted.
  • Final Things: Railings, bannister, or varnish complete the task.

5 Factors That Could Hold Up Your Stair Case Replacement

Replacing a staircase doesn’t always go on schedule. Here are potential hold ups:

  • Design Modifications: Changing the style mid-job brings about delays. Choose early on.
  • Product Hold Ups: Order supplies ahead of time to prevent shortages.
  • Structural Surprises: Schedule an inspection to catch unknown concerns.
  • Workman Availability: Book installers early, specifically throughout busy periods.

Swift and Effective Stair Case Renewal

For a speedier staircase renewal, planning is essential. Consider these tips:

  • Discuss the timetable with your building contractor.
  • Clear the space around the staircase.
  • Consider pre-created designs to save time.
  • Plan temporary access to upper floors.
